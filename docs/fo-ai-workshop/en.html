Claude.ai · 1/3 · Identity

First: tell AI who you are — set your profile once, turn on Memory, and never repeat yourself

Profile (Settings → Customize)
  • Your role & investment mandate
  • Geography and asset class focus
  • Output preferences (format, language, detail)
  • Your work rhythm (daily reviews, markets)
Set once. Every conversation starts in context.
Memory (Settings → Memory)
  • Your views on individual stocks
  • Investment principles & constraints
  • Conclusions from past discussions
  • Research preferences
Grows over time. Tell Claude "remember this."
Without
"Last time we discussed Xiaomi's margin pressure…"
→ AI doesn't remember.
→ Every session starts from scratch.
With
AI knows you follow Asian equities, knows your reporting format, remembers your Xiaomi thesis from last week.

Tool Technique · Perplexity

Structured prompt + verify 3 citations — the two habits that separate good from bad AI research

❌ How most people use it

→ Generic answer, no structure, hard to verify
✅ Structured prompt
"Analyse Xiaomi (1810.HK) heading
into Q4 earnings. Cover: ① Q3 results
summary + key numbers ② EV segment
trajectory ③ Q4 consensus expectations
④ Bull/bear scenarios ⑤ 3 things to watch.
Cite every data point."
Golden rule: when you get an answer, click 3 random citations to verify. This builds your calibration for AI output quality.
